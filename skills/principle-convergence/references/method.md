# Principle Convergence Method

## Direction

This skill moves upward. It uses induction to move from particulars toward a principled ideal. Unlike ADR convergence, it intentionally discards many project-specific details after using them as evidence.

## Evidence Rules

- Do not make a principle stronger than the evidence allows.
- Prefer small principles over broad slogans.
- If research tools are unavailable, mark grounding as `NEEDS RESEARCH` instead of inventing citations.
- If evidence is mixed, narrow the principle until it is defensible.

## Good Principles

A good principle:

- survives outside the original session
- constrains future design choices
- names a mechanism, not a vibe
- can be tested against counterexamples
- is grounded by evidence or an explicit expert premise

## Bad Principles

Avoid:

- generic best practices
- slogans that do not constrain action
- claims that only restate the solution
- principles that hide uncertainty
