# ADR Convergence Method

## Decision Test

A decision is a commitment made against alternatives. It is not merely a claim about the world.

Signals include:

- "we will"
- "go with"
- "not that, because"
- "the rule is"
- an expert closing an open question

Split decisions when they have independent reversal points.

## Particulars Are the Asset

Keep the reason in the expert's framing. Generic ADRs decay because future readers cannot tell what they would give up by reversing them.

Recover:

- context that forced the choice
- options considered
- option selected
- rejected alternatives
- why the selected option won
- consequences and reversibility

## Reconciliation

Use these verdicts:

- **NEW:** no existing decision touches this.
- **REFINES:** sharpens an accepted decision without reversing it.
- **SUPERSEDES:** intentionally replaces an accepted decision.
- **CONFLICTS:** contradicts an accepted decision and intent is unclear.

Flag conflicts. Do not silently resolve them.
